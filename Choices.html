Governments on social media

(The type of social media that are used and why e-governance may be beneficial)

Benefits:

  • Governments using social media are able to implement their policies much more effectively. Hence, increases their influences on their target audience.
  • E-governance brings the citizens and the government together.
  • Provides important information that citizens may need to know in just a click of a button.
  • Increases the participation of the Citizens.
  • Social media is an affordable way to deliver information and communicate with one another.

    • Most Common Used Social Networking Sites:

  • According to Dan and Sahoo, Facebook is in the list of the ten most popular social networking site. (Khasawneh, R. T., & Abu-Shanab, E, 2013).
  • Twitter: another fast growing social media site that is commonly used for -governance. Commonly described as a “large repository of information and as another form of electronic word-of-mouth (e-WOM) that can be used for socio-political purpose and for marketing purpose”

    • Conclusion

    In conclusion from the research we have done we found many pros and cons to governments having a presence on social media, with increased communication at a very low cost but also the possibility of impersonators distributing false information which is why governments need to take precautions when setting up social media accounts.
