Kylie

As a group, we decided to choose the topic of governments on social media. Essentially, this is the Government's use of Social Media to promote and share their information. We decided to choose this topic as social media has grown so much these years and has consumed mostly everyone's lives. With COVID happening, we were updated mostly online by the government which is why this is an interesting topic, as the Government is now appearing online much often.

From our research, we can see that there are so many benefits to e-governance. There are many benefits to e-governance, it allows the Government to share their information, services and enhances their policies to their audience. This is all done by one click of a button for both the Government and the Users and as a result, we are able to retrieve and send information quickly. Social Media also plays an extreme factor for E-governance as the majority of the population consumes these apps on a daily basis. These apps include Facebook, Twitter, Instagram etc., which Governments use to promote their services. With the internet, mostly everyone are able see the latest news, policies and information online

Although these benefits the majority, e-governance has its disadvantages. Evaluating e-governance through our research, I was able to see that the main disadvantages of e-governance is the uprising of cybercrime and technology. Factors that need to be considered is that cybercrime is growing by the minute and hackers are able to leak private data that the Government holds. It is important that the Government ensures data protection as unauthorised access will lead to misuse of their leaked data. According to a journal named “E-Governance in Project Management: Impact and Risks of Implementation,” they recommended that implementing policies and procedures is essential to prevent cyber attacks and should be included in the development process of an e-government system design.

It is also important that the Government acknowledges that not everyone has access to the internet or devices. Solely relying on e-governance would exclude these people from the Government’s services and could cause inequalities. Those who do not have access to the internet will not be able to retrieve important information or services that the Government may provide.
