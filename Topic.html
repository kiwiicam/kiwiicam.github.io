Technology / Topic

Governments on social media

When it comes to free speech, politics and governments we see a lot of governments moving their presence online with government websites and access to plenty of information.

Technology advancements are causing dramatic changes in politics and government. E-Governments use social media as an advantage to improved the communications and wider reach as well as the enhancement of the transparency to the people.

As a result, the Government has recognised the power and importance of social media.Through the internet and social media, the Government is able provide services and information internationally, reaching a large target audience. This enables the Government to receive feedback from different people who express their opinions. (A.Mishaal & Abu-Shana, 2015)

One of the main reasons we chose to do our assignment on Governments on social media is how social media allows the government to engage with the public and how the public would react to certain new laws, or the governments thoughts. Another main reason is how governments use their social media presence in crisis situations to warn and inform people, for example during the COVID-19 pandemic many governments across the world used social media to inform people about lockdowns and COVID-19 statistics.

As the world relies heavily on technology and social media continues to grow everyday, governments joining the trend and hopping on social media was bound to happen. So what are the benefits of Governments using social media, and what are the negatives of them joining? From research undertaken by our group we will analyse all these positives and benefits.

What are the benefits of governments on social media?

The main benefits according to the public spectrum include; better citizen engagement, fast crisis situation communication, testing public perception and accurate information dissemination. Fast crisis situation communication is very important as it means it gets information out to your citizens very quickly allowing more people to get to safety and prepare for what's to come, and with so many people on social media nowadays they are more likely to read something on social media first rather than on the news which is why this it's great that governments are using social media for communicating this way. Better citizen engagement is also a great feature of governments having a presence on social media as with social media it allows people to reply to your posts or tweets which is up for anyone to see allowing users to share their opinion on current issues in the country which the government can see and take in which allows for more informed decisions to be made. Accurate information dissemination allows for the public to get reliable and true information as with many social media accounts posting fake content it can be hard to find true information so with the government on social media it allows them to post content. The malaysian journal of communication has stated the main reason for the government in indonesia joining social media is due to the amount of fake information that is seen on social media in indonesia so with the government joining social media is allows for accurate information dissemination.

According to The malaysian journal of communication governments also use social media to advertise their political campaign. “Since his election to the office in 2014, President Joko Widodo recognizes the importance and relevance of social media to promote the Indonesian government’s image and diplomacy” which just shows how the indonesian government is using social media.

Although there are great benefits to e-governance, there are still negative outcomes to using social media. According to a blog by Legal Wiz, disadvantages to e-governance are loss of interpersonal communication, cost and technical difficulties, illiteracy and cybercrime.

Interpersonal communication is where two or more people are communicating with one another. This could include face to face, video calls and even emails. With social media, interpersonal communication is not possible as it is being broadcasted to essentially the entire world. According to the blog, it is what some people think is an important factor when it comes to politics.

With cost and technologies, according to the blog, it says that there are still disadvantages when it comes to technology. Technology is not perfect, meaning that computers and the internet can go down, preventing services from running. Technology is also expensive making it hard to maintain regularly.

The third disadvantage the blog talks about is illiteracy. Essentially explaining that not everyone is experienced with technology and are not able to operate devices accurately. According to another blog called Myaynan, illiteracy is also another disadvantage. In this blog it explains that if the end user does not understand the technology, people start relying on other traditional methods. Because of this, implementing e-governance will not be helpful if the end user does not fully understand the information given to them.

The last factor given was cybercrime and is a well known disadvantage of e-governance. As technology advances, so does cybercrime. Private data that is stored away from the government is able to be stolen and leaked. Because of this, the Government can lose their supporters as they may lose confidence in the Government's ability to lead their people. According to Myaynan, it is also possible for people to create fake news websites and create misleading information in order to gain benefits. As a result, cybercrime is one of the biggest disadvantages to e-governance.

The science direct website discusses the differences in how different countries governments approach social media and compares the south korean governments use of it to the united states of americas governments use of it and compares them.

Both the U.S. and the South Korean governments look to social media for various functions, which may include, but are not limited to, communication/announcement on current events for the public, such as disease or disaster alerts and weather reporting. Social media encourages open governments; it enhances their ability to reach the digital natives.” Both south korea and the USA do have things in common when it comes to the governments use of social media. The south korean government puts social media tools into 2 different categories, they are either information centered tools which include twitter or Youtube or relationship centered social media tools which include Facebook and linkedin. The U.S on the otherhand uses mainy social media tools but mainly uses social media based on its privacy, digital divide and lack of policy.
